My Name is Alex

I've done some pretty cool stuff in the short time I've had, and I'm proud of what I've achieved.

Army Experience

I joined the Army straight out of high school, in January 2009. Basically I did it because at that stage I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life - and for me that was an unsettling feeling. At the time, I felt like it was a better option compared to enrolling in University, with no idea what line of study I wanted to pursue. In hindsight it was the second best decision I've ever made.

I met awesome people, gained discipline and adaptability, learned how to function under pressure and a whole lot more besides. I also became part of a team, and learned how important team is. The Army taught me that team is an extension of family (that's an IS-A relationship rather than HAS-A)

On top of that, the Army sent me a lot of places to do a lot of things. The photos of Afghanistan that are scattered around my website, were all taken by me! My camera was a potato, even for the time, but I think most of the photos turned out okay!

If you have any questions about my time in the Army, ask away - there are contact links at the bottom of the page

University

Having sorted out a long term plan, in 2013 I decided it was time to leave the army.

I enrolled in a Bachelor of Science, Majoring in Geography and Computer Science. I chose this path because during my time in the army I developed a healthy respect for nature and a love of the outdoors. I want to make sure that, whatever I do, I still make time to enjoy the wilderness around me.

That being said, I also love technology, and believe that technology is going to be of ever increasing importance in the future. The topics I've found most enjoyable at university so far, have been things like web development and design, distributed computing and of course big data and the visualisation of that data. I find algorithms really interesting - my after hours learning is mainly focused around algorithms at the moment and I would love to work somewhere that I could build my skills in things like that.

These two majors are very different fields, but they have a cool intersection, and that is Geographic Information Systems (GIS). Of all things, I'm the most passionate about GIS. I used it extensively in the army and am a firm believer in it's awesomeness. My dream job would be to work at a place where I could put big data on to a map and improve people's lives with the analysis that comes from that. That being said, I have a lot of dream jobs, I'm very open to negotiation!
